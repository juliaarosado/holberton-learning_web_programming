Rating SSBU Stages

banana

Final Destination

A classic. The lack of stage hazards and excessive platforms/structures allow for a fair playing field and the changing background looks sick. A go-to choice for parties or just chilling. 10/10

Battlefield

Another classic, but this time with platforms which aid mobility. Big Battlefield adds platforms which makes playing with 7 other people a lot easier if you can organize 7 people to be in the same place at the same time. I like the plants. 10/10

Bridge of Eldin

Basically Final Destination but with a minor stage hazard. Watching King Bulbin run across the map is all fun and games until you're not paying attention and die. The middle is a pain if you main someone without good recovery or projectiles. 8/10

Smashville

Amazing stage from an amazing game. The characters in the back and the music add nostalgia and seeing the town underneath the stage is a nice touch. It's hard to get the items that spawn on the platform though. 8/10

Temple

A great stage because it caters to a lot of playstyles. For example if you suck and rack up a lot of damage early in the match you can chill in the middle where it's supposedly harder to get KO'd. Another big stage that's good for large groups. 8/10

Tortimer Island

A pretty cool stage, again from a great game. The fact that the layout changes all the time is kind of reminiscent of the way every player's town is different in the series. The fruits and bugs are a nice touch too. 7/10

New Pork City

Another huge stage but it's a lot harder to move around on and the cameras don't zoom well, making it hard to play. I used to be scared of the Ultimate Chimera but now I think it's pretty cute. 7/10

Delfino Plaza

This is a fun stage to play on and look at because of the bright colors and the way the stages are always changing. As an Inkling main, it's not a great stage for me because of all the water but it's definitely one of my favorites. 7/10

Kanto: Pokémon Stadium

This is a relatively neutral stage aside from the transitions that represent different Pokémon types. My favorite part is the jumbotron in the background which shows funny slogans for each brawler. 7/10

Distant Planet

Pikmin was a cool game in concept but I remember getting really frustrated because it was hard so I have beef with this stage. The rain is deadly when it catches you off guard or if someone throws you into it. The Bulborb is too but it's cute so it gets a pass. 6/10

Mario Galaxy

The curve of the stage and the altered gravity is a cool idea until you try to fight on it. This stage is not fun if you are one of those people who rely heavily on projectiles. I like the details of the background and how it brings together details from both games. 6/10

Wuhu Island

I loved Wii Sports Resort so this is one of my favorite stages. The only reason it's kind of low on the list is because in the World of Light fight vs. Iggy the conditions on this stage were a nightmare. 6/10

Wii Fit Studio

A relatively simple stage that is comparable to Final Destination in fairness. It's easy to get confused by the mirrors and self destruct by running off the stage. I like the balance board that runs around. 5/10

Living Room

I don't like stages where things obscure the screen and I'm not a huge fan of the hazards but it's Nintendogs. 5/10

Spirit Train

I don't really like this stage because I'm bad at landing on moving platforms but the colors and graphics are very cute. The fact that Alfonzo drives the train if any variation of Link is fighting is a really nice detail. 4/10

WarioWare, Inc.

This is a fun stage because it's interactive but trying to win the minigames while staying alive is hard. 4/10

F-Zero Grand Prix: Big Blue

I cannot stand this stage. The insanely fast scrolling coupled with the moving platform and the fact that it's basically an instant KO if you fall off the map is ridiculous. 3/10

Mushroomy Kingdom

Again, I don't like scolling stages but this one is a little more tolerable because it's significantly slower than Big Blue. The obstacles can make it hard to keep moving but at least there's solid ground under you. Sakurai has confirmed that he wanted the stage to look like Mushroom Kingdom had been abandoned since it debuted in 1985 and I think that was a nice touch. 3/10

Magicant

This stage is really pretty but the Flying Men that spawn in are a pain to deal with if they're not fighting for you. The colors are cute but the clouds blend into the background so it's hard to tell the difference between the platforms and the air when moving around. 3/10

Saffron City

I don't like this stage because of the Pokémon stage hazards that pop out of the Silph Co. building and can KO you. To avoid them, you're forced to operate on just a few small platforms that hang over empty air. 2/10

Norfair

This stage is really hard to stay alive on because of the excessive stage hazards- during the waves of lava, there's only one completely safe platform to stand on. 2/10

Planet Zebes: Brinstar

Both of Metroid's Planet Zebes stages are really ugly and gross. This one is my least favorite because of the Mother Brain type organism and the acid. 1/10

Moray Towers

An amazing game, but an absolutly awful stage. The original Moray Towers stage from Splatoon was already one of the most widely hated maps in the community and somehow the Smash developers made it even worse. It's nearly impossible to run on thanks to the placement of soft platforms. It's only playable in Battlefiled or Omega form, and the music that plays in the background is its only redeeming quality. 0/10
